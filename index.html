Our Crypt

In the gaslit fog of 1888, Count Aldric Voss opened a dining room where candle-flame never quite reached the corners. MORTIS was not named for death alone — but for the moment when hunger and eternity shook hands across the linen.

London’s whispers became our menu. What was once forbidden became chef’s recommendation. The skull above the mantle is not decoration — it is a former critic, we are told.
